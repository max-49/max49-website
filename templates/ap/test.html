About the Exam

Exam Overview
The AP Schlooth HQ History Exam will test your understanding of the concepts covered in the course units, as well as your ability to analyze primary sources and identify patterns and connections that can support an interpretation of events.

Exam Components

Section IA: Multiple Choice

25 questions 20mins 50% of Score

The questions in the multiple-choice section come in sets of usually 2–3 questions based on the same stimulus. The questions will include one or more sources to respond to such as primary and secondary texts, images (for example, artwork, photos, posters, cartoons), charts, and maps.

You'll be asked to:

  • Analyze the provided sources
  • Analyze the developments and processes described in the sources

Section IB: Short Answer

1 question 10mins 20% of Score

In the short-answer section, you’ll write answers to questions in your test booklet. Some questions include texts, images, graphs, or maps.

  • The question is required, includes 1 primary source, and focuses on developments or processes during the period of March 2021 to February 2022.

You'll be asked to:

  • Analyze the provided sources
  • Analyze developments and processes described in the sources
  • Put those developments and processes in context
  • Make connections between those developments and processes

Section II: Free Response

1 question 30mins 30% of Score

In the free-response section, you’ll write answers to questions in your test booklet.

Document-Based Question
Recommended time: 30 Minutes (includes 5-minute reading period) | 30% of Exam Score

The 30-minute recommended time for this section includes a 5-minute reading period.

  • You’ll be presented with three documents that give various perspectives on a development or process.
  • You’ll be asked to develop and support an argument based on these documents and other evidence from your own knowledge.
  • The topic of the document-based question will include developments or processes during the period of March 2021 to February 2022.
